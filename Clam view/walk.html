Lake Ngā Roto

Ngā Roto is a significant cultural site of importance to iwi and a treasured local community asset just north of Te Awamutu. The boardwalk at Lake Ngā Roto is open to walking, running and cycling activities. Dogs are welcome on a lead. The boardwalk wraps around a natural wetland surrounding the lake and is regularly used by the local rowing club, sailing club, duck hunters, walkers and is a popular tourist attraction.

Walk type: Out and back

Total length: 5.9km

Average time to walk: 1 hour

Accessibility: Very Easy (weather dependent)

Location: State Highway 26 between Hamilton Park Cemetery and Newstead Model School or 126 Vaile Road

Hamilton lake

Hamilton Lake's walk is a tranquil odyssey through nature's embrace. Winding paths lead alongside lush greenery and reflective waters, creating a symphony of serenity. The journey offers a resplendent escape, inviting contemplation and connection with the enchanting beauty that defines this idyllic haven in the heart of Hamilton, New Zealand.

Walk type: loop

Total length: 2.3 Miles

Average time to walk: 35 minutes

Accessibility: Easy (weather dependent)

Location: 64A Lake Crescent, Hamilton Lake, Hamilton 3204

Waikato River Walking

Explore the serene Waikato River with this leisurely riverside walk, perfect for families and dog walkers alike. The trail winds along the riverbank, offering picturesque views of the flowing waters and surrounding greenery. Wooden boardwalks and gravel paths weave through native bush, providing a tranquil escape from city life. You'll encounter charming streams, rustic bridges, and open clearings ideal for a relaxing picnic under the shade of towering trees. Whether strolling along well-maintained pathways or pausing at scenic spots, this walk promises a delightful outing for all

Walk type: Out and back

Total length: 1.4 Miles return

Average time to walk: 35 minutes

Accessibility: Easy (weather dependent)

Location: Waikato River, Hamilton, New Zealand
